# Getting Started with Content Brain

This sets up your own **content brain**: one place on your Mac that holds everything you've ever made, organized, searchable, and in your own voice. It's guided the whole way. You don't need to be technical.

It takes about an hour of your attention spread over a longer stretch (some downloads run on their own while you do something else). You can stop and pick up where you left off anytime.

---

## Step 1: Install Claude Code (one time)

Content Brain runs inside **Claude Code**, a free app from Anthropic.

1. Go to **https://claude.com/claude-code** and follow the install steps for a Mac.
2. Open the **Terminal** app (press `Cmd + Space`, type "Terminal", hit Enter).
3. In that window, type `claude` and press Enter. That starts Claude Code.

If you get stuck here, that's normal. Ask in the group and someone will get you through it.

---

## Step 2: Add Content Brain (one time)

With Claude Code open, paste these two lines, one at a time, and press Enter after each:

```
/plugin marketplace add rolandfrasier/content-brain
```

```
/plugin install content-brain@dragonfly
```

That's it. You now have it.

---

## Step 3: Run it

Type:

```
/content-brain
```

From here, just talk to it. It will:

1. **Check your Mac** and install a few small free tools it needs.
2. **Interview you** about your business, your big ideas, and where your content lives.
3. **Find your content** wherever it is: YouTube, a podcast, a newsletter, your website, files on your computer.
4. **Set up your vault** (a free app called Obsidian) and open it for you.
5. **Pull everything in**, then **transcribe** all your videos and audio into searchable text.
6. **Tag** it all to your own frameworks so nothing is ever lost.
7. **Build your Voice DNA** so anything written from here on sounds like *you*.

It asks before it touches anything. Nothing you own leaves your computer unless you tell it to connect to an account you own.

---

## When it's done

You'll have a complete, private content brain on your Mac, and a summary page telling you exactly what's inside. What you build on top of it next is up to you.
